01 The problem

The moat is rented, and the lease ends at 18

FamApp's moat is built on regulatory arbitrage: minors can't get bank-issued cards. At 18, traditional banks can offer them the exact same product. The core advantage vanishes overnight, creating a built-in retention cliff, a leaky bucket by design.

The reframe

The 18th birthday isn't a churn event.
It's the single highest-intent upsell moment in a user's entire financial life.

At 18, a user becomes eligible for a real account, a first credit card, and a first SIP. FamApp is the incumbent with 5 years of transaction data and an owned PPI license. Don't fight the cliff. Monetise it.

1. The paid option

The teen relationship is a call option on the adult one. FamApp holds 15M of those options, for free.

2. The trigger exists

RBI's KYC jump from ₹10k to ₹2L already forces an upgrade decision. We just have to instrument and convert it.

3. The 2019 vision

Graduating teens into credit and investments was FamApp's founding plan. The solutions below un-shelve it.

Your Financial Identity · private by defaultEverything you built stays, and none of it is visible to anyone unless you choose to share.
  • Auto-off: Parent access turns off automatically at 18. No settings to hunt for.
  • Private by default: Account transitions from shared to 100% private instantly.
  • Opt-in sharing: Users can explicitly invite a parent or friend to view activity.
  • One-tap revoke: View access can be removed instantly at any time.
  • Why it matters: Adults don't want to be watched. Privacy is the trust signal that keeps them on the platform.
  • Trade-off: Parents lose a reason to log in, but we gain the adult user's trust and retention.
  • North Star: % of 18+ users who keep their account private (expected to be high).
  • Guardrail: Privacy-related complaints post-18 (should approach zero).

The fastest legal ship

FamApp registers as an AMFI Mutual-Fund Distributor (ARN, fully online since Oct 2024) and offers SIPs, no demat account required. It stays strictly execution-only (no advice → no SEBI RIA obligation). Stocks/ETFs come later by white-labelling a SEBI broker+DP, integrate, don't build. DigiGold already sits alongside as the ultra-safe entry.

The credibility line: gamify learning badges, never implied gains. That's what separates a wealth app from a trading casino, and keeps regulators comfortable.

  • Execution-Only: AMFI Mutual-Fund Distributor (ARN). No demat needed. No SEBI RIA risk.
  • Vibe Check: 3-tap plain-English quiz ("Steady Grower") instead of jargon-heavy risk profiling.
  • Tiny Starts: Default ₹100 SIP. The "aha" is learning to invest, not gambling.
  • Path: Quiz → Result → Select Fund (low/med ups-and-downs) → Consent → ₹100 SIP live.
  • Empty: "No investments yet, start with ₹100." DigiGold as safer entry.
  • Pending: "SIP starts 1 Jul. Mandate processing <30 sec."
  • Success & Error: "First SIP live" badge. One-tap retry for mandate failures.
  • Edge (Market Dip): Honest framing: "Your ₹100 is ₹98.40 today." Never hide a loss.
  • Revenue: MF distribution trail (ARN), DigiGold spread, future brokerage take-rate.
  • Business Est.: Thin margins initially (~0.5-1%), but AUM compounds. Massive lock-in and cross-sell potential.
  • User: De-risks the scary first-money decision. Early wealth-building in a trusted brand.
  • Guardrails: No F&O or crypto. Gamify learning, never gains. Accept short-term friction for long-term trust.
  • North Star: Investing activation ≤30 days of 18th b'day.
  • Guardrails: First-SIP mandate success, churn spikes during dips.
  • P1: Ship MF-SIP (ARN) with ₹100 default. A/B vs higher defaults.
  • P2 / P3: Layer round-up auto-invest + education badges. White-label broker/DP for stocks later.
